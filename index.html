About Me

Who Am I

I am a Robotics & Control Engineer currently working at DRDO, where I develop intelligent robotic systems for manipulation and interaction using robot kinematics, control theory, and ROS2. My work focuses on closed-loop inverse kinematics, redundancy resolution, force interaction, and motion control for redundant robotic manipulators. Beyond my current research, I am passionate about building intelligent robotic systems by integrating control, perception, computer vision, and autonomous decision-making, with the goal of developing robots that can safely and efficiently operate in real-world environments.

Professional Experience

Senior Research Fellowship

Research & Development Establishment (Engineers),DRDO

September 2023- Present

  • Developed a closed-loop inverse kinematics (CLIK) framework for redundant dual-arm robotic manipulators using the Product of Exponentials formulation.
  • Implemented redundancy resolution techniques including null-space optimization, joint-limit avoidance, and singularity avoidance for smooth and reliable motion.
  • Designed admittance control algorithms for compliant robot-environment interaction, enabling tasks such as door opening and trajectory following under external forces.
  • Designed modular robotics software that streamlined simulation, controller development, and validation, accelerating the development of robotic systems.
  • Performed robot workspace analysis and evaluated manipulator performance through kinematic and simulation-based studies.
  • Currently developing gravity compensation and robot dynamics algorithms to improve motion accuracy and interaction safety.
